"Two Way Mirror Into God's Bathroom" Jason Keller

I have an old whiteboard that I hung by the neck With confessions and letters to myself: “Be still a man” I thought Hume could save me, but I was cause without an effect And still what graced my whiteboard After Hume passed quietly A confession in black ink: “You will always be like this.” Then again in the amber glowing dawn, hair sprouting jets of fire from their roots, from caverns of bone, I stumbled, angry, towards that whiteboard and drew a pen intent to really call myself out: “You won’t get better. Did you even try at all?” I have an old whiteboard that I hung by the neck “I want to be normal” is the last thing it said. Boarded up like a condemned house with wedding invitations, the notes from my friends a birthday card a love letter and a drawing of a squirrel.
