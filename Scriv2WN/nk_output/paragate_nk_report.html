LLM Revision Diagnosis

Length assessment: At 53 short chapters, Paragate is full-length and weighted toward a four-act climax. Overall length is fine; the problem is internal pacing: long, low-energy stretches early and midbook create clear drag corridors that blunt the two-protagonist mirroring gimmick unless trimmed.
Structural health: Structurally strong at the top: the second half — especially chapters 35, 49–51 — supplies clear peaks of action, drama, and thematic payoff. The first third and some midbook scenes sag. Several low-action, high-cut-risk scenes cluster early and in the midbook and threaten momentum.
Primary strength: A tightly built climax cluster (Paragate → Knightfall → Daybreak) that resolves Katiya's arc with high action/drama/theme. The double-protagonist conceit produces powerful mirrored stakes when used to escalate rather than repeat.
Primary risk: Mirror-drag: many mirrored Cody/Katiya beats deliver near-duplicate emotional information rather than additive contrast. Low-energy, high-cut-risk setpieces (dream tourism, domestic replays, procedural domestic days) create long stretches where theme doesn't sufficiently advance the plot.

Keep the major spine and climactic sequence intact — that's where the book sings — but surgically tighten the opening and selected midbook episodes. Eliminate or compress repetitive domestic and ‘tourism’ dream sequences; collapse mirrored scenes unless they show new information or conflicting perspective; protect chapters 49–51 and 35 at all costs. A tight pass focused on merging exposition, pruning fantasy-dream detours, and sharpening the mirrored beats will transform the current slow middle into forward-driving setup for the excellent climax.
